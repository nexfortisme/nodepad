# nodepad — Demo Video Transcript
### "Show Don't Tell" Walkthrough · ~5–7 minutes

---

## Format Guide

- `[ACTION]` — what happens on screen / what the narrator does
- `> text` — spoken narration (sparse by design — the app does the talking)
- `---` — scene cut or pause

---

## SCENE 1 — The Problem (0:00–0:30)

`[Screen: a blank browser tab. Then a Google Doc. Then a Notes app. Then a Notion page. Each briefly visible, then closed.]`

> I have a research problem.

`[Screen: three apps open side by side. A web browser with 14 tabs. A half-finished doc with bullet points. A chat window. Nothing connects to anything.]`

> I capture things everywhere. But I never actually *think*.

`[Beat. Long pause.]`

> The insight I'm looking for is somewhere in the middle of all of this.

`[Screen fades to black. nodepad opens — a clean, dark canvas. The input bar sits at the bottom. Cursor blinks.]`

---

## SCENE 2 — First Note (0:30–1:10)

**Topic: the science of deep attention**

`[The narrator begins typing in the bottom input bar.]`

```
Is the ability to focus deeply a trainable skill — or is it becoming a structural impossibility in modern work environments?
```

`[Press Enter. The note appears in the top-left of the tiling canvas. The header shimmer-animates — a subtle gradient sweep across the tile — indicating the AI is reading it.]`

`[Three seconds pass. The shimmer stops. The tile header settles into a soft violet. The label reads: QUESTION.]`

`[Below the tile text, a faint italic annotation fades in:]`

> *"Bridges cognitive science and organisational design. The distinction matters — a trainable skill implies individual agency; a structural problem implies systemic redesign."*

`[The narrator pauses. Doesn't explain. Lets it land.]`

---

## SCENE 3 — Building Context (1:10–2:15)

`[Second note typed:]`

```
Gloria Mark — UC Irvine researcher. Found it takes an average of 23 minutes to fully regain concentration after a single interruption.
```

`[Enter. A new tile appears beside the first — splits the canvas cleanly. Shimmer. Settles: bright blue header. ENTITY.]`

`[A small dot appears on the QUESTION tile's header. The narrator hovers over it. A tooltip: "Connected to: Gloria Mark." The ENTITY tile dims all other tiles — there are no others yet — and the connection is implied visually.]`

`[Third note:]`

```
"Organisations optimised for responsiveness have structurally eliminated the conditions required for depth." — Cal Newport, Deep Work
```

`[Enter. Shimmer. Emerald green header: QUOTE. The blockquote formatting applies — italic body text with a left accent border in the same green.]`

`[Fourth note:]`

```
https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-022-01340-6
```

`[Enter. Just a URL. The AI identifies it immediately: cyan header, REFERENCE. The annotation reads: "Neuroscience study on sustained attention degradation in high-interruption work environments. Strengthens the Gloria Mark finding with neuroimaging data."]`

`[A source chip appears at the bottom of the tile with the domain name.]`

`[Fifth note:]`

```
The problem isn't smartphones. It's the expectation of instant availability that smartphones made normal.
```

`[Enter. Shimmer. Pink-red header: OPINION. The annotation pushes back slightly: "Worth separating the tool from the norm it enabled — this matters because it shifts where interventions should target."]`

`[The QUESTION tile now shows two dots. Five notes in, connections are forming.]`

---

## SCENE 4 — Editing (2:15–2:40)

`[The narrator double-clicks the body of the OPINION tile. It enters edit mode — the text becomes an inline textarea.]`

`[They add a sentence:]`

```
The problem isn't smartphones. It's the expectation of instant availability that smartphones made normal — and that we've since imposed on ourselves even without them.
```

`[Cmd+Enter to save. The tile re-enriches. Shimmer. The annotation updates.]`

`[The narrator clicks the category badge on the OPINION tile — a small label reading "culture". It becomes an editable input. They change it to "systems". Press Enter. The badge updates instantly — no full re-enrichment triggered.]`

---

## SCENE 5 — More Notes, Richer Canvas (2:40–3:20)

`[Sixth note:]`

```
Build "attention contracts" with yourself — fixed daily windows where all async communication is suspended, non-negotiable.
```

`[Enter. Shimmer. Warm amber header: IDEA.]`

`[Seventh note:]`

```
Deep work: cognitively demanding professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit.
```

`[Enter. Shimmer. Teal header: DEFINITION.]`

`[Eighth note:]`

```
Review Gloria Mark's full research bibliography, read Deep Work chapters 1–3, draft a personal attention protocol
```

`[Enter. Shimmer. The header settles cyan-green: TASK. The text becomes a checklist. Three sub-tasks are automatically parsed — each with an unchecked box. The narrator clicks the first checkbox. It ticks. The text strikes through.]`

`[Ninth note:]`

```
Podcasts mask noise. White noise smooths cognitive load. Silence exposes mental chatter. Three different neurological strategies — same surface goal, different mechanisms entirely.
```

`[Enter. Shimmer. Muted orange header: COMPARISON.]`

`[Tenth note:]`

```
I notice I do my clearest thinking before 8am. That window keeps shrinking — and I've been letting it.
```

`[Enter. Shimmer. Soft lavender header: REFLECTION. The annotation is gentle: "Personal observations about attention windows are often the most actionable data. This one has an implicit call to protect it."]`

---

## SCENE 6 — Synthesis Appears (3:20–3:45)

`[A quiet pulse in the top-right corner. The sparkle icon glows. The narrator clicks it.]`

`[The synthesis panel slides in from the right. A single line of text, rendered large:]`

> *"The scarcity of deep attention is not a personal failure — it is the predictable output of systems that were never designed to protect it."*

`[Below it, smaller: "Emerging from 10 notes · productivity · systems · cognition"]`

`[The narrator reads it. Pauses. Clicks "Solidify."]`

`[The panel closes. A new tile appears on the canvas — distinct gradient header, blue-teal: THESIS. The synthesis is now a first-class note, connected to everything.]`

---

## SCENE 7 — Kanban View (3:45–4:05)

`[Cmd+2. The canvas reshuffles — animated. Tiles group into vertical columns: QUESTION · CLAIM · IDEA · TASK · ENTITY · QUOTE · REFERENCE · OPINION · REFLECTION · COMPARISON · DEFINITION · THESIS.]`

`[The narrator scans across. Each column has its accent colour on the column header. The TASK column shows the checklist state. The THESIS column glows at the far right.]`

`[The narrator hovers the dot indicator on the THESIS tile. All unconnected tiles dim to near-invisible. Only five tiles remain lit — the ones the thesis drew from.]`

---

## SCENE 8 — Graph View (4:05–4:50)

`[Cmd+3. The canvas dissolves. A force-directed graph assembles — nodes drifting into position. The THESIS node settles near the centre, connected to many. The REFLECTION node sits at the outer edge, only loosely connected.]`

`[The narrator watches the simulation settle. No narration. Just the graph.]`

`[They click the ENTITY node — Gloria Mark. The detail panel slides in on the right.]`

`[The panel shows: the full entity text, the annotation, a confidence bar, and a "Connected nodes" section listing QUESTION and REFERENCE with their content snippets. The narrator clicks one — the graph view shifts focus to that node, highlighting it.]`

`[The narrator double-clicks the annotation text in the detail panel. It becomes editable. They add a sentence. Cmd+Enter. The panel updates.]`

`[They click the category badge in the detail panel header. It becomes an input. They change "people" to "researchers". Enter. Done.]`

---

## SCENE 9 — Canvas Index (4:50–5:10)

`[Cmd+I. A right panel opens — the canvas index. All notes grouped by category: "systems (3)", "cognition (2)", "researchers (1)", etc.]`

`[The narrator hovers over "systems". In the graph, three nodes pulse — highlighted in sync. The narrator moves the cursor to "cognition". Different nodes light up. No click required.]`

`[Cmd+I again. Panel closes.]`

---

## SCENE 10 — Command Palette & Export (5:10–5:35)

`[Cmd+K. The command palette opens — a floating modal with a search bar.]`

`[The narrator types "export". Two results: "Export Markdown" and "Copy to Clipboard".]`

`[They click Export Markdown. A download triggers. The narrator opens the file — a richly structured `.md` document: YAML front matter, table of contents, notes grouped by type, claims with confidence tables, sources cited, and a final Synthesis section.]`

`[Back to nodepad. Cmd+K again. This time they type "undo". Click it. The last note disappears. A 2-second toast at the bottom: "Undone."]`

`[Cmd+Z. It comes back.]`

---

## SCENE 11 — Closing (5:35–6:00)

`[Back in tiling view. Cmd+1. The full canvas visible — eleven tiles arranged by BSP split, minimap in the bottom-right corner showing current position.]`

`[The narrator pins the THESIS tile. Its header shifts to a pinned gradient — visually dominant against the others.]`

`[They stop. No more typing. The canvas sits there — a thinking space, a research artefact, a map of a mind working through a problem.]`

`[Slow fade to black.]`

`[End card:]`

```
nodepad
nodepad.space
```

> No chat. No prompts. AI that augments your thinking.
